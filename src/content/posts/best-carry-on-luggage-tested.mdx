The right carry-on luggage decision affects every trip you take. After 50,000 miles testing four carry-ons across domestic and international flights, we have clear picks for three different traveler profiles. The right choice depends on trip frequency, airline preferences, and whether you carry electronics or fragile items. We measured wheel durability, weight, packing capacity, and the practical features that matter on the third day of a long trip.

## What Actually Matters In Carry-On Luggage


<figure style="margin: 1.5rem 0;">
<img src="/images/illustrations/tsv-c1-luggage-compare.png" alt="Hard shell versus soft sided luggage comparison on hotel bed" style="width: 100%; border-radius: 8px; aspect-ratio: 16/9; object-fit: cover;" loading="lazy" decoding="async" />

Four features determine real-world performance. Wheel durability is the most common failure point — cheap wheels develop wobble within 6-12 months of frequent use and become loud and difficult to roll. Quality spinner wheels last 5+ years of heavy use. Handle quality matters because you grab it every time you move the bag — telescoping handles with multiple height positions accommodate different users and tight spaces.

Packing capacity within the size limits depends on the internal frame design. Bags with more internal frame structure protect contents better but limit packing volume. Bags with minimal internal structure pack more but offer less protection. The right balance depends on what you typically carry. Weight matters because airlines weigh carry-ons unpredictably — a bag that weighs 7 pounds empty leaves only 15-25 pounds for contents within typical 22-32 pound airline limits.

## What To Avoid

Three luggage categories should not be your choice. Cheap luggage under 100 dollars typically has wheel failures within 1-2 years — the apparent savings cost more in replacement and frustration. Smart luggage with embedded batteries — airline restrictions now require battery removal making the "smart" features unusable. Luggage sets where carry-on is included with checked bags — usually means lower-quality carry-on construction.

## Trip Length Considerations

Different trip lengths suit different bag sizes.

**Weekend trips (1-3 nights):** Backpack or 17-19 inch underseat carry-on. Some travelers prefer not having to use overhead bins.

**1-week trips:** Standard 22 inch carry-on works well for most one-week trips with packing cubes and one outfit reuse.

**2-week trips:** Standard carry-on plus personal item (backpack or laptop bag). For warm-weather trips with light clothing, this can work even for 2-3 weeks.

**3+ week trips:** Typically requires checked bag plus carry-on. The convenience of carry-on-only diminishes when trip duration exceeds clothing cycle plus laundry availability.
